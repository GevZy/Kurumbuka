Our Conviction

Why we invest in developing
and empowering African leaders

01

The Current System is Broken

We take issue with billions of dollars being wasted on well-intentioned projects conceived by external government agencies and international aid organizations. The current system has fostered a self-perpetuating poverty industry that has sidelined the ingenuity of local leaders in favour of outside experts.

02

It's Sustainable

The quality of local leadership in any low-resource community is a major determining factor in alleviating poverty and creating opportunity. Empowering local leaders to serve their own people proves to be more sustainable, replicable in the long term, and cost-effective.

03

Leaders Multiply Impact

Through over 300 program graduates and stories of community transformation, we are confident that investing in local leaders and helping them multiply impact can help actualize some of Africa's greatest opportunities.

04

Look at Rwanda

We have witnessed the complete transformation of Rwanda from a post-genocide failed state into a bright light for the African continent and the world — due in large part to capable and committed leadership. We believe the same can happen in other African countries.

05

The Impact is Eternal

We know that the harvest is plenty but the workers are few. We want to be part of raising up and empowering Christian leaders in Africa who make an eternal difference by becoming harvesters and creating more harvesters.

Framework

Impact Model

Our Impact Model was developed as a response to the experiences and feedback of local leaders and experts from around the world. It is reinforced by our commitment to biblical truth, servant leadership, an abundant community, and asset-based community development (ABCD).

Etymology

Our Name

Koo-room-boo-kuh

The word Kurumbuka in Kinyarwanda means "to multiply." Its original context is agricultural and refers to planting seeds and producing a harvest. We have adopted it to describe the exciting process that takes place when a local leader brings a compelling vision to reality and multiplies it by training others to do the same. It also represents the organic spread of a great idea from one African community to another.

Background

Our History

Kurumbuka Leadership Solutions brings a bold new vision to a work that began organically in 2015. Our founders developed the Abundant Leadership Institute (ALI) as part of The Wellspring Foundation for Education — led by respected Rwandan community leader Phocas Ngendahayo, carefully selecting 20–25 educational leaders from four countries each year.

As ALI grew and gained a broader vision, it was clear that it needed to be birthed under a separate entity with a mandate focused entirely on developing and empowering African leaders. Kurumbuka Leadership Solutions is now the new home of the Abundant Leadership Institute and the Trek program.

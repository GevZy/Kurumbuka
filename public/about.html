Our Purpose

Why we invest in developing and empowering African leaders.

We have experienced what toxic leaders are capable of — violence, corruption, and exploitation. But we have also experienced what visionary Christ-centered leaders can do. Providing communities with good local leaders is the best and most sustainable way to transform the continent of Africa from within.

01

Leaders shape the present and future of a community or society.

Good leaders bring good outcomes. The quality of leadership in an institution, organization, or community directly determines its health and its potential.

02

The greatest challenge facing Africa is a lack of good leaders.

Despite enormous natural resources, human potential, and economic opportunity, the continent is held back disproportionately by poor governance, corruption, and failed institutions.

03

African problems need African solutions.

Sustainable transformation comes from within. External programs come and go, but well-equipped local leaders stay and build for generations.

04

Christ-centered leadership produces lasting transformation.

Leaders shaped by the values of Jesus — servant-heartedness, integrity, compassion, accountability — build institutions that endure and communities that flourish.

05

Investing in one leader multiplies impact exponentially.

A single transformed leader influences hundreds of lives in their family, organization, and community. The return on investment in leadership development is unlike almost any other intervention.

Our Name Kurumbuka

To burst forth.

Kurumbuka is a Kinyarwanda word that describes what happens when an ordinary person discovers they have something extraordinary to give — and steps forward to offer it. It is the moment a leader emerges. That moment is what we exist to catalyse, over and over, across the continent of Africa.

Our History

From a vision to a movement.

2018

The Vision Takes Root

Founders identify the critical gap in Christ-centred leadership development across Sub-Saharan Africa.

2020

Kurumbuka Founded

The organization is incorporated as a Canadian charity and the first ALI cohort launches in Kigali, Rwanda.

2022

Trek Launches

The 12-week residential program for emerging young leaders begins its first cohort.

2024

19 Nations

Alumni return home to lead change in 19 countries. The Kurumbuka network spans the continent.

Our Foundation

What We Believe

The Bible

We believe the Bible is the inspired Word of God and our ultimate authority for faith, life, and leadership practice.

Jesus Christ

We believe Jesus is the ultimate model of servant leadership — and that following Him is the foundation of transformational leadership.

The Holy Spirit

We believe the Holy Spirit equips and empowers leaders to serve beyond their natural capacity.

Human Dignity

We believe every person is made in the image of God and worthy of dignity, respect, and the opportunity to flourish.

The Church

We believe the local church is central to God's plan for community transformation and is our primary partner in this work.

Reconciliation

We believe that Christ-centered leaders are agents of reconciliation — bringing healing to divided communities and nations.
